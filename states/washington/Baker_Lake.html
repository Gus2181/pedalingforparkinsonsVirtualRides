Checkpoint: Baker Lake

You've made it to Baker Lake!

This lake is situated in the Mount Baker-Snoqualmie National Forest, just southwest of North Cascades National Park. It is primarily fed by the Baker River, though there are several other trebutaries which feed into it as well.

The lake itself covers an area of about 4,800 acres, and can at times hold up to 285,000 acre feet, or 352,000,000 cubic meters of water. The level of water in the lake typically fluctuates around 39 feet annually. However, the lake used to hold less. It was formerly a smaller natural body of water, but was enlarged and raised 312 feet in 1959.

Baker Lake has since become a popular recreational site. People come to fish, camp, and boat on the lake. Most of these people are from Whatcom and Skagit counties, both of which are adjacent to the lake. The area is also home to Swift Creek Campground, which features 55 private campsites for tents or RVs, as well as two group sites, a boat ramp, and a marina. This campground can be found halfway up on Mount Baker Lake, accross from Park Creek, and was formerly known as Baker Lake Resort, and Tarr's Resort (all information courtesy of Wikipedia.com, image courtesy of USGS / Public domain).
