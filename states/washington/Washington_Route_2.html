Route #2 (229.2m)

Welcome to the second route in the state of Washington!

Unlike the first route, which hugged the coastline the entire time, this one starts out close to the shore, but moves inland midway through. This route also features less cities than the first one, which travelled through some of the largest cities in the state, namely Seattle. This route does start in a town, but ends with camping out in a National Park, the North Cascades National Park.

This route totals 229.2 miles, making it middle of the road in terms of length. Some of the distances between checkpoints does start to get fairly sizable, with the longest distance being 87 miles, but none of the checkpoints are ridiculously spread out (image courtesy of National Park Service/Deby Dixon / Public domain).

Checkpoints:

  1. Friday Harbor (44.6m to next)
  2. Skagit Valley (29.4m to next)
  3. Bellingham (68.2m to next)
  4. Baker Lake (87m to next)
  5. Ross Lake (end)
